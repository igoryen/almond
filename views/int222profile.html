Who I am and what my aspirations are

My name is Igor Entaltsev (pronounced EE-gor yen-TAHL-tsev), I am a 3rd semester student at Seneca College of Applied Arts and Technology in the CPD program (computer programmer).

I am an international student at Seneca and I come from Russia. My hometown in Russia is called Sochi and it is located on the Black Sea coast of Caucasus, which is a very picturesque area with the sea and the mountains. Sochi is the warmest place in Russia: we almost never have snow there and I feel very cold in northern parts to great amazement of my foreign friends ("A Russian afraid of snow!")

I went to a public school and to University for 5 years in my hometown and I have a degree in teaching English and German. I have worked as an English teacher for several years though not all the time after my graduation. I worked as a teacher at an American private school in Sochi for a year and I also lived and worked in the People's Republic of China for almost 3 years where I worked as an English teacher in the northern city of Harbin which was a challenging experience for me climatically and emotionally.

After some time in China I found myself interested in going back to college and I chose the area of computer programming. While still in China I bought a textbook on C++ and found it very interesting. I arrived in Canada with my wife and children soon after returning from China in 2011 and I have been a Seneca student since that time.

As to my plans after I graduate Seneca, I intend to work in Canada until I and my family receive a permanent residence status. I hope to find work in the internet area and/or other areas that I have studied at Seneca. Almost all of the subjects I study here are of interest to me, so it is not particularly easy to find the areas that I would like to specialize in. But among the courses that I am taking I find the most exciting are the object oriented programming and the internet. I have had a small encounter with HTML coding 10 years ago and I even created a site for a Chinese friend of mine after reading a short textbook. You could look at the site here. I know it's nothing much, but it was my very first experience back in 2002.
